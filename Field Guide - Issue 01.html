The first cold morning of the year is best spent slowly, by the water.

This week: a botanic garden that's yours if you arrive early enough, an unhurried coffee a street back from the bay, and a craft market on the Williamstown foreshore — this Sunday, and then not for a month.

i. The Morning Shift

Williamstown Botanic Gardens, before the cold lifts.

A formal old garden a street back from the bay, at its best before nine — while the chill is still on the grass and the paths are yours.

This is the coldest weekend of the month, and the Botanic Gardens on Giffard Street are the place to meet it. They were laid out in 1860, among the earliest intact botanic gardens in Victoria¹ — a palm avenue, an ornamental lake, gravel paths even enough for a pram.

Before nine the place is more or less yours: room for the children to run off the early energy, a bench for you. The cafés on the Strand open later, so bring a thermos.

While you're there
  • a.Williamstown BeachFive minutes on foot. Too cold to swim; not too cold to walk.
  • b.Hatt ReserveAdjoining. Open lawn for a ball, when the formal paths feel too composed for a four-year-old.
  • c.Gem PierTen minutes around the foreshore. The ferry comes and goes, and the lobster rolls are worth the walk.
The logistics
  • Where10 Giffard St, Williamstown
  • When8.00 am – 5.30 pm, daily
  • Best atBefore 9.30 am
  • PramYes — wide gravel paths

¹Hobsons Bay Council's wording, and the careful one — several Victorian gardens were established in the 1840s and '50s.

ii. The Afternoon Reset

Kodama, on Stevedore Street.

A small, busy room a few streets up from the water — the warm answer to a cold morning outdoors.

Kodama sits on Stevedore Street, away from the Esplanade and its queue: Axil coffee, a small friendly room, and a menu that runs all day — so a late, cold-walk lunch is no trouble at all.

The order
  • For youA flat white. The beans are Axil.
  • For themAnything off the all-day menu; the point is that there is no wrong hour.
  • WhenAfter the gardens, before the Sunday lunch press

A short, flat walk from the foreshore — close to the water, far from the crowd.

This week's discovery

A craft market on the foreshore, this Sunday only.

The Williamstown Craft Market takes over Commonwealth Reserve on Nelson Place this Sunday, ten until four — handmade and homegrown stalls, food vans, and a small animal farm that will hold a child’s attention far longer than any stall holds yours. It’s free and outdoors; the next isn’t until 17 May, so this Sunday is the only one for a while.

A note in the margin

There is a version of autumn that is mostly admin — the heater serviced, the warmer clothes found at the bottom of the cupboard. And there is the other version: standing in a cold garden at half past eight, noticing the light has changed. We are on the side of the second one.

— Field Guide
