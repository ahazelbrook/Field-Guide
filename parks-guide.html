Field Guide guide · Melbourne inner west

The best parks for kids in Melbourne’s inner west.

We don't list every park. We list the few where the children still talk about it on the drive home.

7 parks Updated June 2026
01 · WILLIAMSTOWN

Williamstown Botanic Gardens, before the cold lifts.

The bay is 200 metres away. The swans are not bothered by you. The garden is formal enough to feel like somewhere, loose enough for a four-year-old. Go early. Bring a thermos. Point Gellibrand is a short walk if you want the water.

Botanic Garden Rd, Williamstown Open daily, sunrise to sunset Williamstown train + 10 min walk

02 · FOOTSCRAY

Footscray Park, once the morning’s quieter duties are done.

A formal Edwardian garden on a river bend that most of Melbourne has never visited. The Maribyrnong slides past on one side; the city is completely invisible. Bring a ball. Walk to the water. Come back for Rudimentary.

Ballarat St, Footscray Open daily Footscray train + 20 min walk, or drive

03 · NEWPORT

Newport Lakes, which does a convincing impression of the country.

A former basalt quarry that has, over thirty years, become one of Melbourne’s better nature reserves. Black swans, a walking loop, and enough tree cover to convince a child they’re somewhere genuinely wild. No café on site — stop at Cornershop first.

Mason St, Newport Open daily Newport train + 15 min walk

04 · ALTONA

Cherry Lake, which used to be a swamp and is now something better.

Five kilometres of flat path around a lake that was drained for an airport that was never built. The birds — egrets, spoonbills, herons — appear to have no idea they’re in an outer suburb. The path is manageable for small bikes. Bezirk is 400 metres from the car park.

Millers Rd, Altona Open daily Drive recommended — 20 min from CBD
Covered in Issue No. 05 → More from Altona →
05 · MARIBYRNONG

Pipemakers Park, a playground built on a hundred years of pipes.

A riverside nature playground on the site of the old Hume Pipeworks — twin pipe slides, rope nests, a sandpit, and a campfire ring marked with Wurundjeri totems. Built on Country, with bluestone ruins and the Maribyrnong River Trail running past. No café on site, so bring a thermos — or roll on to Seddon for breakfast.

32 Van Ness Ave, Maribyrnong Dawn–dusk, daily Free parking + footbridge across the river
Covered in Issue No. 06 → More from Maribyrnong →
06 · POINT COOK · FAR WEST

Point Cook Coastal Park, where the old salt ponds turn pink.

Flat foreshore trails on the western edge of the bay, big birdlife, and the pink salt ponds of the old Cheetham works. Pram-easy and open to the weather — with the RAAF Museum’s hangars a few minutes up the road, and Point Cook Homestead, one of the bay’s oldest farmhouses, nearby. A long-weekend reason to drive west. No café on the foreshore, so bring a thermos.

Point Cook Rd, Point Cook Dawn–dusk, daily Drive recommended — 30 min west of the CBD
Covered in Issue No. 07 →
07 · WEST FOOTSCRAY

Cruickshank Park, a green run-around along Stony Creek.

A long, pram-friendly loop following Stony Creek through West Footscray — open grass for the run-around, a creek trail for the longer walk, and Hansen Reserve a few streets over when you only need fifteen minutes. The reason to bring a thermos and let the morning stretch after the library opens. No café on the path, but Migrant Coffee is minutes away on Barkly Street.

Stony Creek, West Footscray Open daily West Footscray train + 10 min walk
