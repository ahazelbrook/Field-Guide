This week's letter · ANZAC Day

A quiet morning, a riverside park, and forty-two photographs out in the open.

This week: where to mark ANZAC Day with small children, a heritage park along the Maribyrnong, and a new festival that has hung forty-two photographs across the inner west.

i. The Morning Shift

Footscray Park, once the morning’s quieter duties are done.

Where to take small children after ANZAC Day’s observances — a heritage park stepped down toward the river.

ANZAC Day asks something of the morning. The dawn service at the Shrine is a great deal to ask of a four-year-old; the 9.00 am Veterans’ March, with its bands, is the version small children can stand inside¹. Or mark it closer to home — the Williamstown RSL on Ferguson Street holds its own dawn service and gunfire breakfast.

Afterwards, Footscray Park. An Edwardian park stepped down toward the Maribyrnong — heritage elms, a fenced all-abilities playground with a scooter track and water play, the river path running on past it. Room to let the day go quiet again.

While you're there
  • a.The Maribyrnong river pathFlat, paved, prams welcome; the rowers out early.
  • b.Newells Paddock WetlandTen minutes upriver, on the river trail; herons and a boardwalk.
  • c.The view across to FlemingtonThe racecourse, and the city stacked up behind it.
The logistics
  • WhereBallarat Rd, Footscray
  • WhenDaylight hours, daily
  • Best atMid-morning
  • PramYes — paved paths; playground is fenced

¹The march steps off from the corner of Swanston and Flinders Streets and finishes at the Shrine.

ii. The Afternoon Reset

Rudimentary, Footscray — but not before one o’clock.

A courtyard café for a late, unhurried lunch — and on ANZAC Day, late is the only option.

Rudimentary is built around a courtyard, the kind of room that tolerates a pram and a restless child without making a thing of it. One ANZAC Day note: Victoria’s trading rules keep most cafés shut until 1.00 pm on the 25th, so today is a lunch, not a brunch — plan the morning around it. Sunday trades as normal.

The order
  • For youA courtyard table, and an hour you don’t have to hurry.
  • For themThe courtyard does a fair share of the parenting.
  • WhenAfter 1.00 pm Saturday; any time Sunday

Dog-friendly, if yours is the kind of family that includes one.

This week's discovery

Footscray and Yarraville have turned into a gallery.

The In Focus Festival opened on Tuesday: forty-two photographic works on billboards, lightboxes and temporary structures across Footscray and Yarraville — Atong Atem, Simon Terrill and Nicole Reed among the names — with an indoor companion show, The Art of Observation, inside the Footscray Town Hall. Free, self-guided, every day until the end of May.

The details
  • WhereAcross Footscray & Yarraville
  • WhenDaily, until 31 May
  • AgesAll
  • PriceFree

A note in the margin

ANZAC Day sits oddly in a weekend newsletter — a day that asks for stillness, wrapped in a weekend that asks for plans. We don’t think the two cancel out. You can stand quietly at a dawn service and still, by mid-morning, be the parent at the foot of a slide. The day has room for both.

— Field Guide
