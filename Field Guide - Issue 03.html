i. The Morning Shift

Newport Lakes, which does a convincing impression of the country.

A reclaimed quarry turned thirty-three hectares of bushland, ten kilometres from town — and quietest early.

Newport Lakes was a bluestone quarry once. It is now thirty-three hectares of bush: a nature trail, a causeway of stepping stones across the water, more than a hundred and sixty species of bird recorded¹. It does a convincing impression of being a long way from anywhere.

Come early — partly for the quiet, partly because the paths have a few short, sharp hills. A pram will manage; a carrier is a sensible backup. There is a parkrun at eight on Saturdays, if a five-kilometre start to the weekend appeals.

ii. The Afternoon Reset

Cornershop, on the corner of Ballarat and Anderson.

A few steps from Yarraville station, and the zucchini fritters are the reason to go.

Cornershop has an industrial, pop-up feel and a vegetable-leaning menu that changes with what’s good. The zucchini-and-mint fritters are the constant, and the thing to order. It is a short, flat walk from the station — which matters more than it should on a day you have already done a bushwalk.

The order
  • For youZucchini & mint fritters
  • For themThe changing salads are gentler than they sound
  • WhenAfter Newport, before the Village fills up
  • SideAn Industry Beans coffee

Steps from Yarraville station; the flat walk you’ll want after the lakes.

This week's discovery

The In Focus photographs, walked the Yarraville way.

If you missed In Focus last week, or simply want it again: the forty-two photographs are up across Footscray and Yarraville until the end of May. Yarraville Village makes the gentlest loop — flat, compact, a café roughly every fifty metres. Start at the station, walk the works at a child’s pace, and let the stops between them be the point.

A note in the margin

It took a closed quarry forty years to become a forest. Newport Lakes is the kind of place that quietly argues against doing everything quickly — a strange thing to find ten kilometres from the GPO, and a good one.
