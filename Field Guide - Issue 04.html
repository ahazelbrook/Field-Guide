This week's letter · Mother's Day

A museum with a moon in it, and a Sunday that belongs to someone.

This week, for Mother’s Day: a science museum lit by a full-scale moon, a Spotswood café worth the booking, and a run around the Tan that is really a fundraiser.

i. The Morning Shift

Scienceworks, Spotswood, while the weather does what May does.

An indoor morning for a cooling weekend — and there is a moon hanging in one of the halls.

May has turned properly cold, which makes Scienceworks — warm, indoor, ten minutes from most of the inner west — the sensible Saturday. Colour, a touring exhibition from Questacon, fills four halls with twenty-one hands-on things to push, spin and stand inside; it suits roughly five to fourteen.

The reason to go this month is above all that: Museum of the Moon, a seven-metre lunar globe hung in a darkened room. Small children go quiet under it. So, briefly, do their parents.

ii. The Afternoon Reset

The Duchess of Spotswood — book it now, it’s Mother’s Day.

A British-rustic café five minutes from Scienceworks, and the inner west’s worst-kept Mother’s Day secret.

The Duchess of Spotswood, on Hudsons Road, does the treat-tier breakfast — and on Mother’s Day the whole inner west remembers it at once. If this is the plan, ring ahead today; a table on Sunday the 10th will not happen on a walk-in. Five minutes from Scienceworks, which keeps the morning tidy.

The order
  • For youWhatever the kitchen is known for — booking is how you find out.
  • For themThe menu is kind to small appetites.
  • WhenBooked, and early
  • SideA pot of tea, in keeping with the name

Sunday the 10th is the single busiest brunch day of the Melbourne year. Book.

This week's discovery

The Mother’s Day Classic, around the Tan.

A walk or a run by the river on Sunday — four kilometres up to a half marathon, prams entirely welcome. The Mother’s Day Classic has run since 1998, raising money for breast and ovarian cancer research. It starts at Alexandra Gardens and loops the Tan. Built for families: prams welcome, a kids’ zone, food trucks at the end, and The Listies on the main stage.

In short

For when the children are at someone else's place — or asleep.

A note in the margin

Mother’s Day is a strange one to write for: half our readers are buying the breakfast, half are being bought it, and a fair number are doing both before nine. Whichever way it falls in your house — the good version of the day is a small one. A moon in a dark room. A pot of tea. A walk by the river with the people who made you a parent.

— Field Guide
