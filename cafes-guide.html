Field Guide guide · Melbourne inner west

The café guide for Melbourne’s inner-west parents.

Good coffee. Enough space. Staff who don’t visibly tense when a pram arrives.

8 cafés Updated June 2026
01 · WILLIAMSTOWN

Kodama, Stevedore Street.

A small, serious café on a Williamstown side street. Go before ten on a Saturday — it fills quickly and the good seats go first. The kind of coffee that justifies the drive.

58 Stevedore St, Williamstown

02 · FOOTSCRAY

Rudimentary, Nicholson Street — but not before one o’clock.

Worth noting: Rudimentary is a brunch restaurant on weekends, not a café. The kitchen doesn’t open until one. Go for the food, not just the coffee. The room is good. Book if you can.

Nicholson St, Footscray

Covered in Issue No. 02 → More from Footscray →
03 · NEWPORT

Cornershop, on the corner of Ballarat and Anderson.

Exactly what the name promises. A corner café, a good corner, a reliable coffee. Near enough to Newport Lakes to be a useful before or after. Quiet enough on a Saturday morning to actually talk.

Ballarat St & Anderson St, Newport

04 · SPOTSWOOD

The Duchess of Spotswood — book it.

A wine bar and restaurant that happens to serve excellent weekend brunch. Spotswood’s best room. Book it — it fills up. The kind of place that makes a suburb worth moving to.

Hudsons Rd, Spotswood

Covered in Issue No. 04 → More from Spotswood →
05 · ALTONA

Bezirk, where Millers Road meets the beach.

A café at the edge of Altona, where the road ends and the bay begins. The flat white is good. The location is better. Go after Cherry Lake — it’s 400 metres from the car park.

Millers Rd, Altona

Covered in Issue No. 05 → More from Altona →
06 · SEDDON

August, the new corner on Victoria Street.

Eugene Lavery’s first café, in the old Common Galaxia space. Industry Beans in the cup, and cooking a notch above the standard brunch — cauliflower fritters, a broccoli omelette with house-cured trout. Family-friendly without being a play-café compromise. The bracket where breakfast feels, briefly, like a date.

3/130 Victoria St, Seddon

Covered in Issue No. 06 → More from Seddon →
07 · ALBION · FAR WEST

Sadie Black, the Albion corner that feeds you like a friend’s mother would.

Run by locals Chris and Meaghan Blackwell — named, as these places often are, after the dog. The menu is short, seasonal and done properly: the SB Burger regulars press on you, a Ruben that sells out before lunch, and corn fritters with a tomato chutney worth asking extra for. A courtyard for the sun, a cabinet for when it isn’t, and colouring pages on the table. Allpress in the cup. Worth the detour on the way back from Point Cook.

Albion, Melbourne’s far west

Covered in Issue No. 07 →
08 · WEST FOOTSCRAY

Migrant Coffee, four doors from the library.

A bagel-and-coffee shop on Barkly Street, run with more warmth than a Melbourne winter deserves. The order is the Come On — a bagel with fresh sliced ham, the thing that makes you glad you skipped breakfast. The coffee’s good, the room is unfussy, and nobody minds the pram in the corner. Four doors from the West Footscray Library, which is the whole point.

Barkly St, West Footscray
