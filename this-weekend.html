Field Guide · 6 June 2026

This long weekend, go the long way round.

Four things we actually recommend — the airfield at Point Cook, a far-west kitchen in Albion, a Celtic weekend across the bay, and the party closing RISING. For parents with three spare days.

Currently showing Issue No. 07 — the most recent edition.

This week

A long weekend is permission to go the long way round.

Read Issue No. 07 →
  1. 01

    The RAAF Museum, Point Cook.

    The airfield where Australia learned to fly — two hangars of aircraft, free entry, and on Sundays a heritage plane actually takes off at one.

  2. 02

    Sadie Black, Albion.

    A far-west corner that feeds you like a friend's mother would. Allpress in the cup, the SB Burger, and colouring pages on the table.

  3. 03

    National Celtic Festival, Portarlington.

    Open fires on the foreshore, free music and a children's program — best reached by the Docklands ferry, which is half the outing.

  4. 04

    God Save the Queens, Fed Square.

    Or stay in town: a free, all-ages Pasifika takeover closing RISING — and they walk the whole square through the steps from sunset.
