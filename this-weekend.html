Field Guide · 13 June 2026

Deep winter, and a weekend that rewards staying in.

Four things we actually recommend — a warm library in West Footscray, a bagel four doors along Barkly Street, a literary festival by the bay, and a market full of hot donuts. For parents waiting out the cold.

Currently showing Issue No. 08 — the most recent edition.

This week

A weekend that rewards staying in.

Read Issue No. 08 →
  1. 01

    West Footscray Library, Barkly Street.

    The warm-room morning the cold finally justifies — longer Saturday hours, a deep children's collection, a storytime to start, and a coffee that stays hot. Closed Sunday.

  2. 02

    Migrant Coffee, four doors along.

    A bagel-and-coffee shop run with more warmth than the weather deserves. The order is the Come On — a bagel with fresh sliced ham.

  3. 03

    Willy Lit Fest, Williamstown.

    The Town Hall turns over to writers for the weekend, and this year the children's program is free and genuinely good — including an hour inventing characters with Leigh Hobbs.

  4. 04

    Queen Vic Market, the donut sheds.

    Or, if words fail: two sheds given over to hot donuts, mochi and loukoumades, free to wander both days. Go early; bring wipes.
