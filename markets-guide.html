Field Guide guide · Melbourne inner west

Weekend markets in Melbourne’s inner west.

Only the markets worth the drive — and the parking.

3 markets — updated as we find more Updated June 2026
01 · WILLIAMSTOWN

A craft market on the foreshore, this Sunday only.

Held on the Williamstown foreshore — close enough to the bay that you can hear the water between stalls. Craft, ceramics, plants. Arrives a few times a year without much fanfare. Worth knowing about before it’s full.

Williamstown Foreshore Seasonal — check dates Williamstown train + 15 min walk

02 · CARLTON / MELBOURNE

A design market under the dome, with a room kept for the children.

The Royal Exhibition Building is the right scale for a market — grand enough that you feel like you’ve gone somewhere, small enough that you find everything. Ceramics, textiles, prints, jewellery. One room is kept for children’s makers. Worth the trip from the inner west.

Royal Exhibition Building, Carlton Seasonal — check dates Parliament or Melbourne Central + 15 min walk

03 · MELBOURNE · CBD

Queen Vic Market, two sheds given over to hot donuts.

The city counterweight to a bookish weekend: the C–D sheds at the Peel Street end, packed with the makers — hot cinnamon classics, mochi, loukoumades, churro bowls, a few savoury experiments for the contrarians — plus roving performers to keep the sugar moving. Busy, sweet, and entirely free until you start buying donuts, which you will. Go early; bring wipes.

C–D Sheds, Peel St end, Melbourne Sat – Sun, 9am – 4pm · free to enter Flagstaff or Melbourne Central + 10 min walk
