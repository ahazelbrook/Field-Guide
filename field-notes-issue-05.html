The last of autumn by a lake, and a market for when it turns.

This week: a former swamp in Altona that the birds have quietly repossessed, a seaside café at the end of the same road, and a design market under the dome of the Royal Exhibition Building.

i. The Morning Shift

Cherry Lake, Altona, which used to be a swamp and is now mostly birds.

A flat loop around a wetland on the western edge of the bay — at its best early, when the cold keeps the path to yourself.

Cherry Lake was a swamp once — Altona Swamp, on the old maps — until a flood levee in the 1960s gave it an edge and, in time, a purpose. Sixty-odd years on it is thirty hectares of saltmarsh and open water, and the birds have taken it back: black swans, ducks, the occasional purple swamphen stepping through the reeds. A flat, sealed path runs the whole way round¹.

Come early. The reserve is open and unshaded, so a cold autumn morning is felt in full — which is rather the point, and also the argument for the thermos. The path is pram-easy the entire loop, and there is no rule that says you must do all of it. One honest note: the water sits close to the playground at the Millers Road end, so this is a keep-an-eye morning rather than a sit-down-with-the-paper one.

While you're there
  • a.Altona Beach and the pierA few minutes south on the coastal trail. The rebuilt pier is open; too cold to swim, right for a walk.
  • b.Logan ReserveThe foreshore park at the foot of the pier, with Moreton Bay figs big enough to lose a small child behind.
  • c.Pier StreetAltona’s village strip, a block back from the water, for whatever the morning forgot.
The logistics
  • WhereMillers Rd, Altona
  • WhenDaylight hours, daily
  • Best atEarly, before the wind finds the lake
  • PramYes — flat and sealed the whole loop

¹Named for William Cherry, who grazed stock here in the 1840s, when it was less a lake than a swamp — one that, in the hot years, baked dry enough to race horses on.

ii. The Afternoon Reset

Bezirk, where Millers Road meets the beach.

Follow the lake’s own road all the way to the water, and the warm end of the morning is waiting on the corner.

Bezirk is a small room on the corner of the Esplanade, a few doors back from the sand, close enough to the bay that the window does most of the decorating. The coffee is Campos and the chilli scramble is the thing to order; the kitchen is unbothered by a pram, or by a four-year-old who has decided the walk went on too long. Best of all, it is the same Millers Road — followed south from Cherry Lake, it delivers you more or less to the door.

The order
  • For youThe chilli scramble, and a Campos coffee
  • For themThe kitchen will happily scale a plate down
  • WhenAfter the lake, before the strip fills up
  • SideA window seat — the bay does the rest

A flat walk or a five-minute drive from the lake, with the figs of Logan Reserve next door if there is running still to be done.

This week's discovery

A design market under the dome, with a room kept for the children.

Two hundred and fifty independent makers inside the Royal Exhibition Building this weekend — and, just as importantly, somewhere for the kids to be while you look. The Big Design Market’s “Almost Winter” edition runs Friday to Sunday: ceramics, homewares, jewellery, prints — the well-made things you used to have the time to go looking for. Seventy-odd food and drink stalls see to lunch. The staffed kids’ creative space is the Field Notes bit — free activities, indoors, so the looking and the being-four can happen at the same time.

A note in the margin

We had driven past the Cherry Lake turn-off for years before this newsletter finally made us take it. There is a particular kind of place — close enough that you assume you will get to it, and so somehow never do — and a Saturday morning is precisely the thing that rescues it. Consider this your turn-off.

— Field Notes
