i. The Morning Shift

Williamstown Botanic Gardens, at eight in the morning.

A surprisingly quiet spot for a Saturday morning. Enough space for them to run, and enough shade for you to sit down and remember what your own thoughts sound like.

The Gardens sit on the bay edge of Giffard Street, ten minutes from the ferry terminal. They were laid out in 1860¹ — Victorian-formal, gently overgrown in the way the best of the old gardens are — and on a Saturday at eight, they are almost yours. Wide gravelled paths if you're pushing a pram; thick lawn under the Moreton Bay figs if you're not.

We've taken to bringing a thermos of ST. ALi's seasonal blend brewed at home, and a small thing to read. The cafés around the corner — and there are good ones² — open at nine. You may want to slip out for one before you leave. The trick we've come back to is not pretending you'll get coffee with a toddler in tow.

While you're there
  • a. The Time-Ball Tower, Point Gellibrand A five-minute walk; drops at 1.00 pm sharp on weekdays.
  • b. Hobsons Bay foreshore, ten minutes by foot Flat, paved, and the ferry passes every half hour.
  • c. The Cured Cáfé, Ferguson Street Coffee good enough to justify the queue. Open from nine.

ii. The Afternoon Reset

Good Good Burgers, and the case against the kids' menu.

A Yarraville staple that doesn't feel like a compromise. Genuinely accommodating for families, but still a proper treat for the parents.

Good Good is good in the way burgers used to be good, before they had to apologise for it. There is no kids' menu — which sounds inhospitable until you realise it isn't, because what they serve is what kids will actually eat, only better. The classic is twelve dollars. The bun is from Baker D. Chirico. You may want two.

We've started arriving at the soft middle of the afternoon — between the lunch rush and the dinner one — when the room thins out, the staff have time for the kids, and you can hear each other across the table. It feels, briefly, like a date.

The order
  • For youThe Classic. Don't overthink it.
  • For themCheeseburger, cheese pulled aside on request
  • When2 – 4 pm, the quieter window
  • SideSalted fries, share one between two

A small thing — they keep a stack of children's books by the window. Worth knowing.

This week's discovery

A printmaking workshop in Abbotsford, made for families.

A new arts space is running a family-friendly printmaking session every Saturday morning. No experience necessary; the kids design their own and roll the press themselves. The kind of thing that feels less like an “activity” and more like an afternoon you'll actually remember.
